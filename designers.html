Our Designers

Every VELVET VOGUE piece begins in the hands of an independent designer. Meet the ateliers behind the collection.

Partner Ateliers

Six houses, one shared craft

Maison Aurèle
Florence, Italy

Specialists in pure silk draping, Maison Aurèle has hand-finished VELVET VOGUE's eveningwear since 2015, using techniques passed down through three generations of seamstresses.

Atelier Bruyère
Lyon, France

A small tailoring house known for structured outerwear and virgin wool coats, cut and stitched entirely by hand in a converted 19th-century mill.

Studio Lin
Antwerp, Belgium

Champions of Belgian linen, Studio Lin designs relaxed, breathable silhouettes for warm-weather dressing, sourcing every yard within 50km of their workshop.

Casa Merino
Porto, Portugal

A family-run knitwear house producing VELVET VOGUE's cashmere and merino pieces on vintage circular knitting machines restored by the founder's father.

Cuoio & Co
Florence, Italy

Leather goods made using full-grain hides and vegetable tanning, a slower process that Cuoio & Co believes produces bags that age more gracefully.

Maison Solène
Paris, France

A satin and jacquard specialist bringing couture-level finishing to VELVET VOGUE's skirts and eveningwear, founded by a former haute couture patternmaker.
